Lesson for 14-16 Years Old

Welcome to Basic Arithmetic! In this lesson, we will cover the four fundamental operations: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

Addition

Addition is the process of combining two or more numbers to get a total. For example, 5 + 3 = 8.

Subtraction

Subtraction is the process of taking one number away from another. For example, 8 - 3 = 5.

Multiplication

Multiplication is the process of combining equal groups. For example, 4 * 3 means 4 groups of 3, which equals 12.

Division

Division is the process of splitting a number into equal parts. For example, 12 / 3 = 4.

Lesson for 16-18 Years Old

In this lesson, we will delve deeper into arithmetic by exploring more complex problems and their applications in real life.

Fractions

Understanding fractions is crucial. For example, 1/2 + 1/4 = 3/4.

Decimals

Decimals are another way to represent fractions. For example, 0.5 + 0.25 = 0.75.

Percentage

Percentages are used to express proportions. For example, 50% of 20 is 10.

Word Problems

Applying arithmetic to solve word problems helps in understanding real-world scenarios. For example, if you buy 3 apples at $2 each, the total cost is 3 * 2 = $6.

Lesson for University Level

At the university level, arithmetic forms the foundation for more advanced mathematical concepts. Here we will explore some of these concepts.

Algebraic Manipulation

Understanding how to manipulate algebraic expressions is key. For example, simplifying (3x + 2) - (x - 4) = 2x + 6.

Complex Numbers

Complex numbers extend the concept of one-dimensional number lines to the two-dimensional complex plane. For example, (3 + 2i) + (1 + 4i) = 4 + 6i.

Matrix Arithmetic

Matrix arithmetic is used in various applications like computer graphics and solving systems of equations. For example, adding matrices A and B involves adding their corresponding elements.
