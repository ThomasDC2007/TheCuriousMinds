Stephen Hawking: Exploring the Universe

Stephen Hawking, born in 1942 in Oxford, England, was a renowned theoretical physicist and cosmologist. Despite facing severe physical challenges due to ALS, Hawking made groundbreaking contributions to our understanding of the universe's origins and black holes.

Stephen Hawking at a lecture

Hawking's most notable work includes the discovery that black holes emit radiation, now known as Hawking radiation. His bestselling book "A Brief History of Time" brought complex scientific concepts to a wider audience, making him a global icon in science and a role model for overcoming adversity.
