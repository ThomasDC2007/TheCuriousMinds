Lesson for 14-16 Years Old

Welcome to Algebra! In this lesson, we will cover the basics of algebraic expressions, equations, and inequalities.

Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions are combinations of variables, numbers, and operations. For example, 2x + 3 is an algebraic expression.

Solving Equations

To solve an equation, find the value of the variable that makes the equation true. For example, solving 2x + 3 = 7 gives x = 2.

Inequalities

Inequalities compare two expressions. For example, 2x + 3 > 7 is an inequality.

Lesson for 16-18 Years Old

In this lesson, we will delve deeper into algebra by exploring more complex equations and their applications in real life.

Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation is an equation of the form ax² + bx + c = 0. For example, solving x² - 4x + 4 = 0 gives x = 2.

Factoring

Factoring is breaking down a complex expression into simpler factors. For example, factoring x² - 4 gives (x - 2)(x + 2).

Functions

Functions represent relationships between variables. For example, f(x) = 2x + 3 is a function.

Lesson for University Level

At the university level, algebra forms the foundation for more advanced mathematical concepts. Here we will explore some of these concepts.

Abstract Algebra

Abstract algebra studies algebraic structures like groups, rings, and fields. For example, understanding the group of integers under addition.

Linear Algebra

Linear algebra deals with vectors, vector spaces, and linear transformations. For example, solving systems of linear equations using matrices.

Complex Numbers

Complex numbers extend the real numbers and are used in various fields. For example, solving x² + 1 = 0 gives x = ±i.
